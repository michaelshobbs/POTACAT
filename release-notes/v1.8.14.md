# The "Oops, I Started my FT8 Reply a Little Late but It Still Works!!" Release

**Headline:** v1.8.14 teaches JTCAT to **start an FT8 reply late and still get it through** the way WSJT-X does, and adds the receive-side mirror — **a priori (AP) decoding** — so weak and late answers to *your* CQ get pulled out of the noise. Plus the **Yaesu FT-710**'s antenna tuner over CAT, a clearer per-radio audio setup, and arm64 Linux downloads.

## ⏱️ Late-start FT8 transmit (WSJT-X parity)

Tap a reply a few seconds into the cycle — chasing a spot, reacting to a late decode — and JTCAT no longer makes you wait for the next slot. It now does what WSJT-X does: it sends a **truncated waveform**, skipping the leading symbols and starting mid-transmission while staying locked to the symbol clock, so the tail still lands on the normal interval boundary.

The full, error-corrected message is always sent — nothing is shortened or stripped of FEC; only the on-air waveform is clipped. The receiving station re-syncs on the middle and end Costas sync arrays, and because the tail finishes on time, your PTT releases cleanly without bleeding into the next slot. FT8-only; replies up to ~7 seconds late still make it out. *Proven on the air — a 5-second-late reply completed a full QSO.*

## 📡 A priori (AP) decoding — recover the weak and late answers

The receive-side companion to late-start TX. When a station answers your CQ weakly, or late with its leading sync already gone, the normal decoder can't read it. AP decoding runs extra LDPC passes that hypothesize your own callsign — and, once you're in a QSO, the station you're working — to pull those messages out, the same asymmetric trick WSJT-X uses to dig deep. The 14-bit CRC still has to check out, so it never invents a contact. Recovered decodes get an **AP** badge in the JTCAT decode list. On by default; just set your callsign.

## 🎚️ FT-710 antenna tuner over CAT (#55)

The Yaesu **FT-710**'s built-in ATU can now be tuned remotely — the Tune button is back in the rig panel. Its firmware rejects the documented tune command, but **AC003;** (Tuner Activate) runs a full tune cycle from either an off or on state, with no preliminary command needed. Works over both direct serial and rigctld. — thanks **baumertjohn** for tracking down the right command and confirming it.

## 🔊 Clearer per-radio audio setup

The audio input/output device pickers moved out of a buried "ECHOCAT Audio (optional)" disclosure into a front-and-center **Audio Devices** block in each rig's settings, with plain "RX audio in / TX audio out" labels — these drive FT8, SSTV, **and** ECHOCAT, not just remote audio. When your audio source streams straight to the radio (SmartSDR Direct, Icom Network), the device pickers now tuck themselves away, since there's no soundcard in that path. New FlexRadio rigs default to SmartSDR Direct so FT8 works with no DAX program to set up. Existing audio configurations are carried forward untouched.

## 🐧 Linux: arm64 downloads on the website (#43)

The download page now offers **both x86_64 and ARM64** Linux builds — AppImage, .deb and .rpm — with the architecture clearly labeled, so Raspberry Pi and SBC users get the right build and amd64 users are never handed an ARM image by mistake. — reported by **Sleuth56**

---

### Notes
- Late-start TX and AP decode are both **on by default** and FT8-specific. Toggle them in the JTCAT popout as **Late TX** and **AP RX**.
- AP decoding only attempts recovery on candidates the normal decoder couldn't read, so the extra cost per cycle is small.
