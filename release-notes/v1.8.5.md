# Tap-to-pair + Tailscale host propagation

**Headline:** v1.8.5 lands the desktop half of two long-asked-for mobile flows. **Tap-to-pair** lets you skip the QR scan entirely on your home LAN — tap your desktop in the iOS app, click Approve on the popout that appears here, done. **Tailscale-host propagation** quietly hands your tailnet hostname to every paired phone so a phone that paired at home automatically falls back to Tailscale when it leaves home — no re-pair needed.

## Tap-to-pair

The home-LAN pair flow no longer requires a QR scan. When the mobile app sees a POTACAT desktop on the local network (via mDNS) and the user taps the discovered card, the phone POSTs to a new endpoint on the desktop and the desktop pops a small **Approve / Deny** window:

```
Casey's iPhone wants to pair with this station

  📱  iOS · from 192.168.1.142
  [▓▓▓▓▓▓░░░░] Expires in 47 s

  ▸ Show this station's cert fingerprint

  [ Deny ]            [ Approve ]
```

- Click **Approve** → desktop mints a device token, appends it to your paired-devices list, hands the credentials back to the phone, and the phone connects normally. Same end-state as the QR pair flow.
- Click **Deny** → desktop responds 403, phone surfaces "the owner denied the pair request." No paired record is created.
- 60-second auto-deny if you don't click anything — protects against modal spam from a forgotten request.
- If POTACAT is minimized or in the tray, the desktop is brought forward AND an OS notification fires so you actually see the request.
- The popout also exposes the station's TLS cert fingerprint behind a disclosure, for paranoid users who want to compare it against the QR-style fingerprint or `~/.config/potacat/cert.pem`.

**Concurrency**: only one pair request can be pending at a time. A second device requesting while the popout is open gets `pair_request_busy` (503) and is told to try again. Safer than queuing.

**Allow tap-to-pair from the local network** toggle under Settings → ECHOCAT → Advanced → Connection. **Defaults on.** Turn it off if you share your network with strangers (libraries, coffee shops) — POTACAT will refuse pair-request POSTs without ever showing the modal.

**Cloud Tunnel exposure always blocks tap-to-pair**, regardless of the toggle. A stranger on the public internet hitting your tunnel hostname would otherwise be able to spam the Approve modal on your desktop. The QR pair flow (`/api/pair` with a single-use 32-byte hex token) stays open over the tunnel — that's how you pair when you're not on the LAN.

The new endpoint is `POST /api/pair-request` with body `{ deviceName, devicePlatform, requestId }`. The phone holds the HTTP response open for 60 seconds while waiting for the operator's click; on Approve it gets the same `PairResponse` shape that `/api/pair` returns (`deviceToken`, `deviceId`, `fingerprint`, `protocolVersion`, `serverVersion`, `tsHost`, `cloudHost`).

## Tailscale host propagation

A paired phone now learns about your desktop's alternate hostnames automatically so it can fall back when the primary host stops resolving.

- **`tsHost`** — your desktop's Tailscale MagicDNS name (e.g. `shack-mac.billfish-noodlefish.ts.net:7300`). Detected via `tailscale status --json`, recomputed on startup, on every Cloud-Tunnel state change, and every 10 minutes.
- **`cloudHost`** — your `<callsign>.potacat.com` hostname (when POTACAT Cloud Tunnel is active). Already in the QR; now also flows over the wire.

Both fields ride on:

- The `auth-ok` message that every phone receives at connect time → already-paired phones pick up the new value without re-pairing.
- A new typed `alt-hosts` push message → fires immediately when the value changes mid-session.
- The QR payload — first-time pairs on the LAN get `tsHost` baked into the stored device record.
- The `/api/pair` response — QR-redeem flow.
- The new `/api/pair-request` response — tap-to-pair flow.

The mobile side will use these as a fallback chain: `device.host → device.tsHost → device.cloudHost`. Each leg fails fast on DNS NXDOMAIN / connection refused. The cert pin from the original pair applies to all three hostnames (same self-signed cert across all of them), so no new cert work on the phone.

## Why this matters

- **95% of home-LAN first-pairs don't need a QR anymore.** Two taps end-to-end: phone tap + desktop Approve.
- **Tailscale "just works" after setup.** Add Tailscale to phone and desktop, connect to POTACAT once at home — from then on the phone automatically uses the tailnet when away. No "edit your paired device" step that nobody finds.
- **No paywall regression.** Cloud Tunnel still wins on UX (no Tailscale setup, no MagicDNS config) and stays the headline paid path. Tailscale users get a free, working away-from-home story.
