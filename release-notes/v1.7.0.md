# Flex Direct — No SmartSDR Required

**Headline:** POTACAT now drives a FlexRadio all by itself — tune, listen, and transmit with **no SmartSDR and no DAX running at all**. It registers as the radio's own GUI client over the native API, and everything that used to need SmartSDR in the loop — spot tuning, the rig panel, RX audio, CW, the panadapter spots — works standalone. The FlexRadio settings got a ground-up rebuild to match, the WinKeyer now keys a network Flex, and the Tiles on the Air integration got a lot lighter on its host.

## Flex Direct — POTACAT *is* the Flex client now

Until now POTACAT rode along with SmartSDR: it needed SmartSDR running to be the radio's "GUI client," and it tuned through SmartSDR-Win's CAT shim on TCP 5002. Close SmartSDR and POTACAT went deaf — `tune ignored — no radio connected`.

v1.7.0 changes the model. When POTACAT connects to your Flex's API on port 4992 and finds no GUI client, it **registers as one itself**. The radio's band persistence hands it the slice, and from there POTACAT tunes natively. No SmartSDR, no DAX program, no CAT shim.

What works standalone now:

- **Tuning** — click a spot and it tunes; frequency and mode track every QSY.
- **The rig panel** — ATU, NB, filter, RF gain, S-meter, SWR.
- **RX audio** — hear the radio through your computer. Open the VFO popout and click the speaker button. It plays the VITA-49 stream POTACAT pulls straight off the radio — the Windows DAX device stays out of it entirely.
- **CW** — keyed through the SmartSDR `cwx` path, authorized because POTACAT is the GUI client.
- **Panadapter spots** — pushed over the same connection.

And it adapts on its own:

- **Auto-discovery** — a "Discover radio" button in the rig editor finds your Flex on the LAN by listening for its UDP broadcast. No typing IP addresses.
- **Mid-session handoff** — open SmartSDR an hour into a session and POTACAT hands the radio back to it; close SmartSDR and POTACAT reclaims it. Automatic, both directions.

This also lays the groundwork for operating a Flex remotely over the internet — that's the next phase.

## A FlexRadio rig panel built around the radio, not the plumbing

The old Flex settings were scattered: the connection on the Radio tab, "push spots to panadapter" (with its *own* duplicate IP field) on the Spots tab, audio source on the ECHOCAT tab. v1.7.0 consolidates everything Flex into one place — **Settings → Radio → your Flex rig**:

- **One "Radio IP" field.** The duplicate host setting is gone; an existing config is migrated automatically.
- **The connection is just the IP.** "FlexRadio (SmartSDR)" is now plain "FlexRadio"; the legacy TCP 5002–5005 slice picker moved to an "Advanced" disclosure as a fallback.
- **Panadapter spots** is a simple checkbox — it rides the same connection, so there's no IP to enter.
- **Audio source** (DAX vs. SmartSDR Direct) lives on the rig, with a live connection-status line.

## WinKeyer paddle CW to a network Flex

If your WinKeyer is plugged into the PC by USB rather than wired to the radio's key jack, paddle CW never reached a network Flex. Now POTACAT enables the WinKeyer's paddle echo and relays each decoded character to the Flex's `cwx` keyer. Paddle a CQ and it goes out — verified on the air, with RBN spotting the call. The "CW (?)" status badge also no longer cries wolf when POTACAT is itself the GUI client.

## VFO popout: a VOL control, and a frequency that keeps up

- A **VOL slider** below the S/SWR/ALC/TX meters throttles the radio audio monitor independently of your PC's master volume. 0–150%, persisted across restarts.
- The frequency readout now **tracks every QSY** under Flex Direct. The native API has no CAT poll to echo frequency back, so the popout, the main window, ECHOCAT, and the N1MM broadcast are all updated the moment you tune.

## Tiles on the Air — much lighter polling

The tilesontheair.com operator pays the Supabase bill out of pocket and flagged POTACAT's request volume. Two fixes:

- POTACAT now fetches the **full active snapshot** instead of incremental `since=` deltas — which also fixes Tiles spots vanishing from the table a few seconds after they appeared.
- The Tiles poll is **rate-limited to once per 20 seconds**, decoupled from your spot-refresh interval.

## Other improvements rolled up

- **Main-process memory leak fixed** — POTACAT could run out of memory after ~46 minutes on SmartSDR Direct / Flex audio. Audio frames were being forwarded as plain arrays and overwhelming the garbage collector; they stay as typed arrays now.
- **TX EQ** — custom EQ sliders, a TX VU meter, and per-rig EQ defaults in the VFO popout and the ECHOCAT bridge.
- **Raspberry Pi** — the `alsa_native` addon is now built on Ubuntu 22.04 so it loads on Pi OS Bookworm; a 24.04-built v1.6.1 binary required a newer glibc.
- **SSTV** — fixed a detached-ArrayBuffer crash on every SmartSDR Direct / K4 audio frame while the SSTV decoder was running.
- **ECHOCAT** — the spot-row "worked" dimming now uses a today-only QSO summary; failed pairing attempts are logged with a reason.
- **Wavelog** — upload failures now surface the real rejection reason instead of an opaque "abort."
