# Initial WSPR framework and more robust logging

**Headline:** v1.8.15 lays the groundwork for **WSPR** inside JTCAT — beacon transmit, the spot panel, the propagation map, band hopping, and even driving the beacon from your phone — and adds **much more detailed CAT logging** to make rig-control problems diagnosable. Plus a Kenwood data-mode TX fix, an IC-7300 PTT reliability fix, and JTCAT polish.

## 📡 WSPR — initial framework (preview)

WSPR ("whisper") joins **FT8 / FT4 / FT2** in the JTCAT mode dropdown. Selecting it swaps the QSO panel for a dedicated WSPR panel built for a propagation-beacon workflow:

- **Beacon transmit** on the standard WSPR sub-bands, hard-capped at **1 W (QRPp)**, with a **TX% scheduler**, power (dBm) selection, and the 2-minute RX/Beacon/TX countdown. The transmit waveform is a clean-room, fully validated encoder.
- **Spot panel + map** — time, SNR, drift, frequency, call, grid, distance and bearing, with DXCC entity lookups, a DX highlight, and SNR-colored map markers with great-circle lines to your QTH.
- **"Where am I heard"** footprint, **band hopping** to sweep the beacon/RX across bands, and **WSPRnet** upload of decoded spots.
- **Drive it from your phone** — the WSPR beacon and panel are wired over the ECHOCAT network.

> ⚠️ **Receive/decode is not active in this build yet.** WSPR reception relies on a bundled decoder that isn't shipped in v1.8.15, so the spot list will stay empty for now — **beacon transmit works**, receive/decode lands in a follow-up. This is the "initial framework" — the plumbing, UI, transmit path, and network layer are all in place.

POTACAT itself stays fully open and **Apache-2.0 licensed**; the WSPR decoder is kept as a separate, independently-licensed program rather than linked into the app.

## 🪵 More robust rig logging

The CAT log now records **what your radio reports** — its frequency and mode on every change — and, for Icom CI-V rigs, the **raw frequency frame** (command + addresses) behind each reading. If your displayed frequency ever disagrees with where the radio is actually tuned, a bug report now captures exactly what the radio is sending back, instead of leaving it a guessing game. (Driven by an IC-7100 frequency-readout investigation — thanks **K6RBJ**.)

## 📻 Rig control fixes

- **Kenwood TS-590S/SG, TS-890S, TS-990S — digital TX now uses the rear/USB audio input.** POTACAT was keying these rigs with the plain `TX;` command, which transmits from the **front mic** — so FT8 and SSTV keyed the radio but sent dead air (the rig just beeped). These models now key with the data-send `TX1;` command, routing your computer audio through the rear/ACC/USB input as intended. — serial-logger-confirmed by **KF0WXX**
- **Icom IC-7300 — more reliable PTT in SSB-over-DATA.** The mode-change and PTT commands were sent back-to-back, and on Icom's CI-V bus the radio could drop the second one, leaving the radio in DATA mode but never actually keying. The PTT key-down is now staggered after the mode change so it doesn't get lost.

## 📻 JTCAT polish

- **Day/night terminator on the JTCAT map** — the same grayline overlay the Propagation map has, so you can see where it's dark at a glance.
- **My Activity feed is more compact** — the timestamp now sits inline on each row instead of on its own line, so more of your exchange is visible at once.
- **"PTT: CAT / VOX" indicator** now follows the app theme correctly (it was hard-coded and looked wrong in the light themes).
- **Full Auto CQ** auto-places your TX on a detected quiet frequency.
