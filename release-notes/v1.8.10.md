# ECHOCAT Auth Updates

**Headline:** v1.8.10 fixes the tap-to-pair failure where the desktop shows the Approve popup, you click Approve, and the phone still says **"Network error reaching the desktop"** and never pairs.

## 📲 Tap-to-pair now survives a dropped connection

While the phone waits for you to click Approve, it holds a connection open to the desktop — and iOS likes to kill idle connections faster than a human can walk to the keyboard (flaky LAN links, bridges, and mesh nodes make it worse). When that happened, the desktop minted the pairing credentials on Approve but had nowhere to deliver them: the phone errored out and the approval was silently lost. If your network dropped that connection every time, pairing was *impossible* — and your desktop's paired-device list quietly collected ghost entries from each approved-but-undelivered attempt (feel free to Revoke those).

The desktop now keeps every Approve/Deny outcome retrievable for two minutes, and a retry from the phone picks up exactly where it left off — same approval window, no second popup, no "another pair request is pending" error. Until the matching ECHOCAT app update ships its automatic retry, simply **tap Tap-to-Pair again right after an error** and the pairing completes. — **K6RBJ**, whose shack desktop lives behind a microwave LAN bridge and could never finish pairing

**Tip for tunnel users:** if your POTACAT Cloud Tunnel is on, the pairing QR pairs through the cloud and skips the LAN entirely — scanning the QR is the most reliable path on tricky networks.
